# Password spraying (T1110.003)

A small password list (often 1–3 common passwords) tried against many
users. Looks the opposite of T1110.001: low-rate per account, high
breadth. Often spread across many source IPs to dodge per-IP rate
limits.

## Triage

- Are the failed-login source IPs in many `/24`s? That's the spraying
  signature.
- Check whether the targeted accounts share a property (department,
  role, recent breach dump appearance).

## Investigation

- Audit any account that succeeded — high chance of compromise.
- Pull the password complexity policy and look for gaps (passwords
  matching the season + year, etc.).
- Correlate with `intel_hits` — sprayers often reuse rented IPs that
  appear on AbuseIPDB / known-bad lists.

## Containment

- Enable conditional-access challenges for the affected app for 24 h.
- Force MFA enrolment if not already mandatory.

## Eradication & lessons

- Postmortem the password complexity policy.
- Tune the SIGMA rule threshold based on observed spraying rate.
